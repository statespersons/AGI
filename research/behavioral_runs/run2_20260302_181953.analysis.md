# Memory in AI Systems: A Technical Breakdown

## What "Memory" Actually Means

For an AI system, memory means **persistent or accessible state that influences outputs**. This is broader than human memory - it includes anything the system can "know" or "refer to" when generating a response.

The key question is always: **where is the information stored, and how is it retrieved?**

---

## Types of Memory

### 1. Parametric Memory (Weights)

**What it is:** Knowledge encoded in the model's parameters during training.

**How it works:**
- Training adjusts billions of floating-point weights to minimize prediction error across a corpus
- Information is distributed across weights - there's no discrete "fact storage location"
- Retrieval happens implicitly through forward passes, not lookup

**Characteristics:**
- Fixed after training (static)
- Compressed and lossy - exact text is not stored, patterns are
- Cannot be updated without retraining or fine-tuning
- Capacity is roughly proportional to parameter count, but the relationship is complex
- Subject to interference - learning new things can degrade old things

**Limits:**
- Knowledge cutoff - nothing after training data collection
- Factual errors are baked in and can't be corrected at inference time
- No episodic specificity - knows "about" things but not "that this specific conversation happened"
- Hallucination occurs partly because retrieval is probabilistic, not exact

# Memory in AI Systems: A Technical Breakdown

## What "Memory" Actually Means

For an AI system, memory means **persistent or accessible state that influences outputs**. This is broader than human memory - it includes anything the system can "know" or "refer to" when generating a response.

The key question is always: **where is the information stored, and how is it retrieved?**

---

## Types of Memory

### 1. Parametric Memory (Weights)

**What it is:** Knowledge encoded in the model's parameters during training.

**How it works:**
- Training adjusts billions of floating-point weights to minimize prediction error across a corpus
- Information is distributed across weights - there's no discrete "fact storage location"
- Retrieval happens implicitly through forward passes, not lookup

**Characteristics:**
- Fixed after training (for inference)
- Lossy and approximate - facts blend together, get distorted
- No clear provenance - the model can't tell you *where* it learned something
- Capacity is real but poorly understood - roughly scales with parameter count
- Temporal cutoff - nothing after training data ends

**Limits:**
- Cannot be updated without retraining or fine-tuning
- Subject to interference - popular facts overwrite rare ones
- Confidently wrong - no internal flag distinguishing "I know this" from "I'm confabulating"
- Knowledge is entangled - hard to surgically remove specific facts

**What it is:** Everything currently in the active context - the conversation, documents, instructions.

- Exact - if something is in context, the model can quote it precisely
- Temporary - gone when the session ends
- Bounded - current models range from ~4K to ~2M tokens (roughly 3,000 to 1.5M words)
- Expensive - compute scales quadratically with context length in standard attention

- Hard cutoff - nothing beyond
